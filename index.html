Pocket M8

End-to-End UX Case Study · Social Support Platform

Role: UX/UI Designer Platform: Web App Timeline: Multi-phase

Overview

Pocket M8 is a digital social-support platform designed to help users manage tasks, events, and communication in sensitive, trust-driven contexts.

The product was live but suffered from unclear flows, weak feedback states, and high cognitive load. The goal was to improve clarity, emotional safety, and usability without disrupting real user workflows.

Research & Context

Users & Context

  • Clients working with social workers
  • German-speaking users
  • Mixed digital literacy
  • Often interacting under stress

Key UX Challenges

  • Confusing task vs event distinction
  • Silent failures in messaging
  • Unclear permissions and role context
  • Overloaded dashboards

Design Process

The redesign followed an iterative process focused on real constraints and emotionally safe interactions.

Key Screens Designed

  • SMS & magic link authentication
  • Automatic role detection (client / social worker)
  • Clear task vs event creation
  • Explicit messaging unavailable states

Visual Design

The UI prioritises calm layouts, soft contrast, clear typography, and predictable patterns to support users under stress.

Outcomes & Learnings

  • Improved clarity across core workflows
  • Reduced user hesitation during key actions
  • Stronger trust through explicit feedback
  • Higher stakeholder confidence in usability

Key Learnings

  • Silent failures damage trust more than visible errors
  • Emotional clarity is as important as functional clarity
  • Constraints often lead to better UX decisions
