Designing a Client–Social Worker Communication App for a Switzerland-Based Organization

Product: PocketM8 · Role: UX/UI Designer · Platform: Mobile App (Client Side)

PocketM8 is a mobile application designed for clients of social workers to communicate, receive tasks, manage events, and share files in a safe and structured way.

The product is owned by Sozial Digital, a Switzerland-based organization serving German-speaking users.

The project involved redesigning and refining an existing app, improving usability, reducing confusion, and making the experience intuitive for users with varying levels of digital literacy.

  • My Role: UX/UI Designer
  • Duration: Initial design + 5-day usability & iteration cycle
  • Target Users: Clients of social workers (German-speaking, Switzerland)

Problem Statement

The existing PocketM8 app functioned well technically, but usability gaps became evident after real-world usage.

  • Multiple login methods caused role confusion (clients vs mentors)
  • Task and event states were not visually clear
  • File interactions lacked clarity, especially around downloads
  • Interaction patterns such as long-press actions were inconsistent
  • Dense layouts reduced readability for non–tech-savvy users

Because the app is used by clients who may already be under cognitive or emotional load, clarity and predictability were critical.

Users & Context

The target audience consists of clients working with social workers in Switzerland. Users have mixed levels of digital literacy and often interact with the app under stress.

  • High importance of clarity and trust
  • Actions like accepting or rejecting tasks must feel safe and reversible
  • Tasks often relate to health, administration, or daily life

Research & Discovery

Instead of theoretical research, this project relied heavily on practical usability testing and real-world observation.

  • Review of existing wireframes and specifications
  • Hands-on testing of the live app
  • Direct feedback from the product team
  • Observation of real usage scenarios

Usability Testing (Live App)

I tested the live production app by simulating both roles: social worker (web app) and client (mobile app).

  • SMS login
  • Email login
  • Magic link login
  • Messaging
  • Tasks & events (accept, reject, complete)
  • File upload and download
  • Profile and avatar editing

Design Process

Step 1: Testing & Documentation

  • Tested all major flows
  • Documented friction points directly in Figma
  • Flagged UX vs technical issues

Step 2: Refinement & Redesign

  • Improved layouts, spacing, and alignment
  • Introduced consistent card patterns
  • Refined micro-interactions
  • Reduced ambiguity in states and actions

Step 3: Feedback & Iteration

  • Two rounds of structured feedback
  • Adjustments based on product and user insights
  • Final polish for developer-ready handoff
