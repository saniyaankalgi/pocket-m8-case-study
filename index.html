Designing a Client–Social Worker Communication App

UX/UI Designer · Mobile App · Switzerland · German-speaking users

PocketM8 is a mobile application designed for clients of social workers to communicate, receive tasks, manage events, and share files in a safe and structured way.

The project focused on redesigning an existing app to improve usability, reduce confusion, and support users with varying levels of digital literacy.

Problem Statement

While the app functioned well technically, real-world usage revealed usability gaps including role confusion, unclear task states, inconsistent interactions, and dense layouts.

Users & Context

The primary users are clients working with social workers in Switzerland. Many interact with the app under stress, making clarity and predictability critical.

Research & Discovery

Rather than theoretical research, the project relied on hands-on usability testing using real flows and live app behavior.

Usability Testing

Testing covered authentication, messaging, tasks, events, file handling, and profile management across both client and social worker roles.

Design Process

The process followed three phases: testing and documentation, refinement and redesign, and structured feedback leading to a developer-ready handoff.
