Pocket M8

End-to-End UX Case Study · Sozial Digital

Role: UX/UI Designer Platform: Mobile Application Type: Live Product Redesign

Project Overview

Pocket M8 is a digital social-support platform developed under Sozial Digital, designed to help users manage tasks, events, and communication within a sensitive and trust-driven context.

The product was already live but faced usability breakdowns, unclear flows, and friction during onboarding, messaging, and task or event creation.

My role was to audit, redesign, and structurally improve the experience while respecting real user behaviour and technical constraints.

Problem Statement

  • Unclear onboarding and entry points
  • Confusing task vs event creation flow
  • Poor feedback states, especially in messaging
  • Lack of clarity when a contact became unavailable
  • Overloaded dashboards with weak hierarchy
  • Missing emotional cues in sensitive interactions

Goals & Success Criteria

  • Reduce cognitive load across key flows
  • Create clear system feedback and states
  • Remove role-related confusion
  • Design for emotional clarity and trust

Research & Discovery

Research relied on live product testing, heuristic audits, stakeholder input, and scenario-based walkthroughs rather than formal interviews.

Users & Context

  • Clients working with social workers
  • German-speaking users
  • Mixed digital literacy levels
  • Often interacting under stress

Key UX Challenges

  • Ambiguous task vs event distinction
  • Silent messaging failures
  • Inconsistent interaction patterns
  • Unclear permissions and role context

Design Process

The redesign followed a continuous improvement loop focused on testing, redesigning, and iterating with real constraints.

Key Design Decisions

Tasks and events were explicitly separated and supported with contextual descriptions to reduce ambiguity.

Messaging unavailable states were clearly communicated through disabled inputs, explanatory text, and visual cues.

Visual Design

The visual language focused on clean layouts, soft contrast, clear typography, and familiar interaction patterns to support emotionally sensitive use cases.

Outcomes & Impact

  • Improved clarity across core workflows
  • Reduced user hesitation
  • Stronger emotional alignment with user needs
  • Positive stakeholder feedback

Learnings

  • Silent failures damage trust more than visible errors
  • Consistency builds confidence
  • UX in sensitive domains requires empathy

Future Improvements

  • Formal usability testing
  • Accessibility audits
  • UX metrics and analytics
  • Design system documentation

Final Note

This project strengthened my ability to design responsibly for real users, collaborate with stakeholders, and prioritise emotional clarity alongside functional usability.
