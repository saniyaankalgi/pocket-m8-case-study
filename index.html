Designing a Client–Social Worker Communication App

UX/UI Designer · Mobile App · Switzerland · German-speaking users

PocketM8 is a mobile application designed for clients of social workers to communicate, receive tasks, manage events, and share files in a safe and structured way.

The product is owned by Sozial Digital, a Switzerland-based organization serving German-speaking users.

  • Role: UX/UI Designer
  • Duration: Initial design + 5-day usability & iteration cycle
  • Platform: Mobile App (Client side)
  • Target Users: Clients of social workers

Problem Statement

The existing PocketM8 app functioned well technically, but real-world usage revealed several usability gaps.

  • Multiple login methods caused role confusion
  • Task and event states were not visually clear
  • File interactions lacked clarity
  • Inconsistent long-press interactions
  • Dense layouts reduced readability

Since users often interact with the app under stress, clarity and predictability were critical.

Users & Context

The primary users are clients working with social workers in Switzerland, with mixed levels of digital literacy.

  • High importance of clarity and trust
  • Users may open the app under stress
  • Actions must feel safe and reversible

Research & Discovery

This project relied on hands-on usability testing rather than theoretical research.

  • Review of existing wireframes
  • Live app behavior testing
  • Product team feedback
  • Real usage observation

Usability Testing
